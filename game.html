    TIP

    Reading the wind

    The arrows show where the wind blows from.

    Within 38° of head-to-wind you are in the no-go zone and lose speed fast.

    The wind keeps shifting up to 30° around its base direction.

    sailing strategy guide

    Three things to know before your first race

    Start position, wind shifts and right-of-way calls matter as much as raw speed.

    01

    Don't cross the start line early

    Build speed below the line during the countdown and cross northward right after the START signal. Crossing early means OCS — you must return below the line.

    02

    Avoid the no-go zone and trim your sail

    Pointing straight into the wind kills your speed. Sail about 45° close-hauled with the sail in, and ease it out when heading downwind.

    03

    Round marks wide, counter-clockwise

    Every mark must be rounded counter-clockwise following the ↺ arrow. Hitting a mark costs a one-turn penalty, so enter wide and smooth.

    frequently asked questions

    FAQ

    Can I play without installing anything?

    Yes. Open the page in a desktop browser like Chrome, Edge or Firefox and play immediately.

    Why do the boat colors change during the race?

    When the boats get close, green marks the right-of-way boat and red the boat that must keep clear.

    Is this exactly the same as real racing rules?

    It is a simplification for learning and gameplay. In real races, always check the current RRS and sailing instructions.

    Can I play on mobile?

    The game is optimized for two players on one keyboard. Touch controls may come later.

    simplified racing rules

    Rules applied in the game

    RRS 10

    Opposite tacks

    Port tack keeps clear of starboard tack.

    RRS 11

    Same tack, overlapped

    The windward boat keeps clear of the leeward boat.

    RRS 12

    Same tack, not overlapped

    The boat behind keeps clear of the boat ahead.

    RRS 13

    While tacking

    A tacking boat keeps clear of the others.

    RRS 14 / 31 / 44

    Contact & penalties

    Boat contact costs two penalty turns; touching the RC, pin or a mark costs one turn.

    This game is an educational simplification. In real racing, serious damage or advantage may not be exonerated by turns, and sailing instructions may change the penalties.
