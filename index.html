WHY RIGHT OF WAY MATTERS

Who keeps clear? The question every sailor must answer in seconds.

In dinghy sailing, two boats meet dozens of times per race — and every time, the Racing Rules of Sailing decide who holds course and who keeps clear. Port tack gives way to starboard (Rule 10), windward keeps clear of leeward (Rule 11), the boat behind keeps clear of the boat ahead (Rule 12), and a tacking boat keeps clear of everyone (Rule 13).

Dinghy Lab turns those rules into things you can see: move the boats and watch the decision change, then test yourself in a real race against a friend. Made for sailing club beginners, updated for the 2025-2028 rulebook including the revised mark-room rule.
