      3-SECOND CHEAT SHEET

      Decide in 3 seconds on the water

      Ask just three questions, in this order. It settles most situations.

      1. 1

        Are the boats on different tacks?

        Port tack keeps clear. (RRS 10)

      2. No, same tack ↓
      3. 2

        Are they overlapped side by side?

        The windward boat keeps clear. (RRS 11)

      4. No, not overlapped ↓
      5. 3

        Then they are bow to stern.

        The boat behind keeps clear. (RRS 12)

      ⚠️

      If a boat is tacking

      The tacking boat keeps clear — always. (RRS 13)

      🟡

      Near a mark

      Inside the 3-length zone, mark-room rules apply on top. (RRS 18)

      🤝

      Even with right of way

      Everyone must avoid contact. (RRS 14-16)

      RULES 10-18 · RRS 2025-2028

      The rules this tool covers

      Simplified from Part 2 of the World Sailing Racing Rules of Sailing 2025-2028.

      RRS 10

      Opposite tacks

      A port-tack boat shall keep clear of a starboard-tack boat.

      RRS 11

      Same tack, overlapped

      A windward boat shall keep clear of a leeward boat.

      RRS 12

      Same tack, not overlapped

      A boat clear astern shall keep clear of a boat clear ahead.

      RRS 13

      While tacking

      After passing head to wind, a boat shall keep clear until she is close-hauled.

      RRS 14

      Avoiding contact

      Every boat shall avoid contact if reasonably possible.

      RRS 15

      Acquiring right of way

      A boat acquiring right of way shall initially give the other boat room to keep clear.

      RRS 16

      Changing course

      A right-of-way boat changing course shall give the other boat room to keep clear.

      RRS 17

      Proper course

      A boat with a leeward overlap from clear astern shall not sail above her proper course.

      RRS 18

      Mark-room (2025 update)

      Overlapped at the zone: outside gives inside mark-room. Not overlapped: first to the zone gets it.

      Out of scope: complete mark-room decisions, obstructions, room to tack (Rules 19-20), starting situations, team racing.
